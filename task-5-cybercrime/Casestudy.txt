Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal personal information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites.
For example, a college student receives an email that looks like it is from a bank. The email says that their account will be blocked unless they update their details immediately. It also contains a link. When the student clicks the link, it opens a fake website that looks exactly like the real bank website. The student enters their login details, thinking it is safe. However, the information is sent to the attacker.
Phishing attacks usually target students, working professionals, and people who are not aware of online frauds. The consequences can be serious, such as loss of money, hacked accounts, or misuse of personal information.
This type of cybercrime is increasing because it is easy for attackers to create fake messages and websites. Therefore, awareness and careful online behavior are very important to stay safe.
